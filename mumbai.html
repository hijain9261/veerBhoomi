Mumbai

Stories of Freedom

Mumbai, India’s commercial powerhouse, was a crucible of freedom. From trade unions to political uprisings, the city’s streets and ports resonated with the cries of independence.

Leaders, activists, and ordinary citizens shaped the fight for independence, proving Mumbai’s heart beat for freedom.

Notable Freedom Fighters

B. R. Ambedkar

Leader, social reformer, and champion for equality and justice.

Gopal Krishna Gokhale

Moderate leader who shaped early Indian political thought and reforms.

Dadabhai Naoroji

‘Grand Old Man of India’, campaigned for India’s rights in British Parliament.

Key Events in Mumbai's Freedom Struggle

  • Swadeshi and boycott movements in city markets and mills.
  • Labor strikes and protests in ports and factories.
  • Participation in Non-Cooperation and Quit India Movements.
