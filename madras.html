Madras

Stories of Freedom

Madras (now Chennai) was a center of political activism, social reform, and nationalist movements in southern India. Leaders from this region played crucial roles in India’s independence struggle.

From early reformers to revolutionary figures, Madras contributed immensely to shaping the freedom movement in the South.

Notable Freedom Fighters

C. Rajagopalachari

First Indian Governor-General of India and a prominent freedom leader.

E. V. Ramasamy (Periyar)

Social reformer and activist advocating equality and anti-caste movements.

Subramania Bharati

Poet and nationalist whose writings inspired resistance and unity.

Key Events in Madras's Freedom Struggle

  • Participation in Non-Cooperation and Civil Disobedience movements.
  • Formation of local Congress units and social reform movements.
  • Promotion of education and anti-colonial literature in Tamil Nadu.
